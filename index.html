How I work as:

A Leader

I am happy to lead a team of developers or embark on a solo development project. Communication with the client here is key. Client-to-lead-developer communication is one of my main focuses when I accept a lead position to ensure the vision a client has is what they receive when I submit a final product.

When working with a team, I like to reassure everyone that I am no more than another teammate, who is more than eager to help my future teammates out. I like to explain my leader-position as only helping to organize the team so that everyone can play to their strengths. This method of leadership allows for a tighter-knit team where every team member can feel equally valued and comfortable voicing their thoughts, ideas, and concerns.

A Team Member

I always give my best efforts to encourage a tight-knit, communicative, and efficient team. Communication amongst teammates is cruicial in the development of the highest quality of tools. While I always ensure I thoroughly complete my work first, once my tasks have been completed, I genuinely enjoy turning to my teammates to see what work I can help them with. I am never affraid to ask my teammates for help, and present myself as readily available for my teammates at the first sight of trouble.

The team that is most-efficient is a team of individuals whom each play to their own strengths. This being said, I am not afraid to take on a task I am unfamiliar with should my teammates and I feel that I am best equiped for the job amongst my teammates. I am also perfectly happy to learn new languages, techniques, and tools in order to help my teammates with their coding issues if need be.
